02 Splitting time too evenly between alliances Strategy ▼

Splitting time between CO2 and SVL is actually a solid strategy — it's consistently gaining us an average 2-minute lead every rally cycle. The problem is that one #1 error wipes out 10 minutes in a single swing, making the 2-min gains irrelevant.

Goal: find a way to stack more time into the main alliance so we build a lead that can absorb mistakes.

Status

◆Needs simulation work. No one-size-fits-all answer but there are situational strategies worth having in our back pocket. More on this soon.

For the nerds Optimisation problems I want to work through

This castle seriously broadened my view of what's possible when you treat the whole thing as an optimisation problem with pre-planned situational strategies. If anyone has the brain for it and wants to discuss — I'd love to run stuff by you.

1
The 2-alliance time allocation model How do we maximise time stacked in the main alliance while keeping the secondary alliance functioning? There's almost certainly a more optimal split than what we're doing — and probably situational variants we should have ready to deploy. Needs simulation.
2
Rally sequencing edge cases The current sequence is clean when it works. But what are the failure modes? What does the enemy have to do to break it, and what's our counter? I want to map these before we face a smarter opponent.
3
The Helios problem Our Helios count is genuinely felt on the battlefield. What's the minimum viable Helios distribution across CO2/SVL to make a meaningful difference? Is there a coordination play here for dolphins who are deciding what to build next?
4
Zero-risk time gains There are probably small coordination tweaks that gain us 30–60 seconds per cycle at near-zero risk. I want to catalogue these. Small things compound over a long SVS.
